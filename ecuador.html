Good to Know

📋 Before You Go — Book These Early

Sea Lion Yacht — Bartolomé
Dec 12 (Saturday). Operated by Finch Bay Hotel. One of only 12 vessels with a Galápagos National Park day-tour permit. Max 20 guests, 2 naturalist guides, onboard chef. Book well in advance.
Sea Lion Yacht — North Seymour
Dec 14 (Monday). Same operator and vessel as Bartolomé. Book at the same time.
Agora — Los Túneles
Dec 16 (Wednesday). $164/pp · max 10 guests · lunch included. Best snorkeling in the archipelago — small boat, fills fast.
Agora — Tintoreras
Dec 17 (Thursday morning). $68/pp · max 16 guests. Book together with Los Túneles.
Yellow House Birding Reserve
Dec 10 early morning, Mindo. $95 for two · 3.5 hrs with guide. Private 200-hectare reserve — book directly with the lodge.

✈️ Quito Airport — Galápagos Flight Day

⚠️ Arrive at the airport at least 2 hours before your Galápagos flight. You must visit the CGREG counter (TCT validation) AND the SICGAL luggage inspection booth before you can check in. Both queues take time.
Have ready: digital TCT on your phone (or printout), passport, and round-trip Galápagos ticket. After TCT validation, go to SICGAL for the physical bag inspection — no prohibited foods, plants, or seeds. Only then can you check in.

⛴️ Inter-Island Ferries

Both crossings (Santa Cruz → Isabela on Dec 15, Isabela → Santa Cruz on Dec 17) take about 2 hours and can be rough. Sit on the center or lower deck, bring a light jacket, and have snacks and water. Take any motion sickness medication before boarding, not after it gets rough.
⚠️ Bags are inspected every time you move between islands (biosecurity). Remove any organic material — fruit, plants, seeds — before each crossing or you'll be asked to dispose of it at the dock.

🏖️ Tortuga Bay (Dec 13)

Sign in at the National Park booth at the trailhead — required, takes 2 minutes. The popular approach: walk one way (1.5 miles, 45 min, paved and flat through cactus forest) and take the water taxi back (~$10/pp, ~20 min). Water taxi departs Puerto Ayora at 9 AM & 2 PM; pickups from Tortuga Bay at 2 PM & 5 PM — buy tickets at the kiosk by the pier.
⚠️ There are zero facilities or vendors at the beach. Bring all water and food you'll need — the equatorial heat and the walk will drain you faster than you expect.
The long beach (Playa Brava) is for wildlife only — don't swim there. Walk to the far end through the mangroves to reach Playa Mansa, the calm lagoon where you can snorkel. Sea turtles and rays are common.

🌿 Quito Teleférico

Go in the morning — skies are typically clear early and cloud up in the afternoon. The cable car rises from 9,350 ft to 13,000 ft; bring a jacket even if it's warm below. The ride takes about 10 minutes each way.

🤿 Snorkeling Gear

Reef-safe sunscreen only — regular sunscreen is prohibited in Galápagos waters and will be confiscated. Apply before you leave the hotel, not at the beach. A rash guard is better than sunscreen for long snorkel sessions anyway. Bring an underwater camera or GoPro — you'll want it at every site.

🌡️ Weather — December

December is the start of the warm/wet season in the Galápagos. Expect warm, sunny days (75–85°F) with occasional brief showers. Water temperature is warm (~72°F) — a shorty wetsuit is optional but comfortable for long snorkel sessions. In Quito (9,350 ft elevation) it's cooler — 55–65°F, bring a light jacket. Mindo cloud forest is warm and humid — 65–75°F with frequent mist.
